History Of Temple

1. 17.07.1968 - Shri Manohar Lal Jain, Chief Plant Engineer, B.H.L. A Jain Parishad was formed here, in which Prof. Khushhal Chand Jain Gorewala from Varanasi was also present. 

2. From 17 to 19.05.1969 - Inauguration of Muni Shri 108Pantandandak Ji Maharaj in Jwalapur-Kankhal. On 18.05.1969, BHEL Jain Samaj got Muni Shree's diet done at BHEL, Sector-1. Maharaj encouraged the construction of a Jain temple in Shri Nebhel Colony. 

3. From 1969 to 1974 - On the occasion of Dasalakshini, Nivaran Utsav and Mahavir Jayanti, the Jain family of BHEL Colony, B.H.E.L. Used to go to Jwalapur and Kankhal Jain temples by bus. 

4. 1970 - BHEL Gave an application to the manager for allotment of land for the construction of a Jain temple. 

5.11.12.1970 - B.H.E.L. The Management wrote to the Council to get it registered with the Registrar of Firms and Societies. 

6. 28.09.1974 - Shri & Ph. Manjain Parishad, B.H.E.L. The registration number 1443 was received by the Registrar of Firms and Societies. 

7. In Panchpuri from November 1974 to November 1975 - 2500th Nirvana year of Tirthankar Mahavir Bhagwan was celebrated at the national level. Shree Vam-Manjain Parishad took out a tableau on 08.04.1975 at Jain Temple Haridwar on the occasion of Mahavir Jayanti. 

8. December 1974 - BHEL The manager allotted land for Jain temple near Goga Baba temple. This place was in a pit, next to a rain drain and in front of an earthen mound and two to two and a half kilometers away from the colony. Therefore, Mr. and Fun-Manjain Parishad refused to take it.

9.Janveer 1975 - Shri Pham and Jainman Parishad corresponded several times to give another land. 

10. 20.03.1978 - New site shown in Sector-4 for construction of Jain Temple, which was accepted (Ministry of Heavy Industries, Delhi accepted it on 17.09.1981 after much correspondence).

11. From December 1978 to March 1979 - Two rooms in the courtyard were completed. 

12. March 1979 - In - Jambu Dweep, Hastinapur in Pachan Kalyan, a Shresthi took three and a half feet of marble Bhagwan Shri Mahavir Ji's Siddhasan statue to B.H.E.L. Got the Jain temple consecrated.

13.20.10.1979 - Brought the idol from Hastinapur and made it sit in a room and rituals started being performed daily in the Chaityalaya. Along with it there was also a small metal idol of Lord Chanda Prabhu. 

14. From 1978 to 1980- B.H.E.L. Architect Mr. V.K. Gupta went to the Jain temples of Modinagar, Saharanpur, Meerut, Delhi etc. to understand their structure and architecture along with Mr. Dinesh Chanda, Minister, Mr. and Fan-Manjain Parishad.

15. October 1979 - Mr. Dinesh Chandra, Professor Dr. O.P. Jain Head Civil Engineering Department, should have darshan of the statue in the womb. So that one can understand its broad form. 

16.November 1979 - Mr. Dineshchandra B.H.E.L. K D.G.M. (Project Civil) Mr. P.C. Rao was encouraged to design a 50x50 feet hall without pillars and a Jain temple with an open view of the sanctum sanctorum. Which Mr. P.C. gladly accepted.

17. September 1980 - On the occasion of Dashalakshini in Chaityalaya, called P. Devi Prasad Jain Shastri of Saharanpur and took advantage of the discourse. 

18. June 1982 - Shri P.C. Rao has drawn the shikhara / sanctum sanctorum of the grand temple and the hall map without pillars with eight steps, which is R.C.C. The base was made according to the design and given. All the work was done free of cost.

19. October 1982 - The working drawing of the temple map was prepared (this was also free of cost). 

20.According to the measurement by an architect, the model of the temple was made of cardboard about 18 inches in height free of cost, carrying which he used to go to meet people to collect donations.

21. In the surrounding cities, the process of asking, writing letters and meeting the relatives and acquaintances of the members to donate for the construction of the temple was started. At that time there were 127 members of the Shri Wa Pham-Manjain Council. The estimate of the temple was around Rs 15 lakh.

22. November 1982 - A civil construction company, which also used to build the temple, was contracted for this work. 

23. 19.01.1983- Excavation was started by marking the four pillars of the sanctum sanctorum. 

24. 25.01.1983 - Danveer Seth Shri Shikhar Chand Jain of Meerut performed Bhoomi Pujan and construction of Shikhar started. 

25.08.01.1984- The construction of Shikhar and sanctum sanctorum was completed, along with the pillars of the hall also started to stand.

26. April - May - June - 1984 - Due to the very unique design of the hall with eight initiatives and simultaneously R.C.C. There was a huge challenge in terms of base construction. In this Mr. Dinesh Chandrao Mr. Chandra Jain, Manager Civil had special cooperation on quality control. The concreting of the roof took 72 hours in which 6 mixers were used continuously. During this, the day and night presence of Member Mr. Rajendra Kumar Jain, Deputy Manager, Welding Technology, BHEL and Mr. Himanshu Jain was special.

27. April-May 1985- Concrete work of Hall and Shikhar completed. 

28. October 1987 - The interior work in the temple is almost completed and it was decided that from 16.01.1988 to 23.01.1988 Shri Adinath Jinbimb Panch Kalyank should be done. 

29. 20.12.1.87 Shri Gurnam Sharan, E.D. and Mr. A.D. Panch Kalyanak Magazine was released by Parashar, GM, HEAP, Haridwar. 

30. January 1988- The work of Panduk Shila and Mandir ji was completed. 

31. From 16 to 23.01.1988 1 The metal idol of Lord Shri Adinath in the temple was consecrated in Panch Kalyanka and the transfer of the idols from Chaitalya to the sanctum sanctorum took place on 23.01.1988, the last day of the grand Panchkalyan festival. 

32. The main details of Panch Kalyank are as follows:- The entire work was done under the aegis of Todermal Memorial Trust, Jaipur. 
Pratishtacharya of Panch Kalyank - P. Gyan Chand ji had come from Vidisha.
3. In Panch Kalyank, worship was done in the presence of Vadacharya Shri 108 Shanti Sagar Ji Maharaj, Upadhyay Shri 108 Chandra Sagar Ji Maharaj, Alec Shri 108 Nirvana Sagar Ji Maharaj. 
3 pp. Panch Anuvrat - Aunt Asha Ram ji, Saharanpur wore it.
3. Main speakers of religious discussion:- P. Hukm Chand ji Bharill, Jaipur, P. Kanu Bhai Ji Shah, Dahod, P. Poonam Chand Jichhabra, Indore.
3. The rain of flowers from the sky was done by Delhi Gulf Airways Service Pvt.
3. Sangeet and Bhajan Sandhya was organized for the first time in Haridwar city. In which the whole auditorium kept clapping due to the presentation of Shri Ravindra Jain and Anuradha Paudwal.
3 Kalash Rohan on Appan Shikhar - done by Mr. Satish Chand Jain, Jwalapur. 

33.From 1989 to 1992 - The remaining work of the temple was completed. For the safety of the temple, a high wall was built around it. 

34. 25.12.2002 to 29.01.2003 - Boundary wall in front of Panduk Shila started from digging foundation on 25.12.2002 and completed on 29.01.2003. The foundation stone of which was laid by Mr. Bhagmal Jain, father, Mr. Santosh Kumar Jain, ED, BHEL, Haridwar and Mrs. Ila Jain, Mr. Vijay Kumar Jain, GM, BHEL, Haridwar, whose entire expenses were borne by the management committee of that time.

35.April 2003 to May 2003- Acharya 108 Shree Paantanthanlchham Sanmati Sagar Maharaj arrived in Bhel Jain Temple, in which Maharaj encouraged the people of the society to rebuild Shree Neshikhar, which was built by Shree Balesh Jain, Shree J.C. Haridwar, Sripavan Kumar Jain, K-69 Shivalik Nagar and done with the money given by other members. The pinnacle of Shikhar was done on 23.05.2003 by Shri Ashok Shastri Prakritacharya Sanghastha Acharya 108 Shri Paantanthanlchch Sanmati Sagar Maharaj.

36. 2008 - Sadhu Bhavan with two rooms was built in the premises of the temple with the donations given by Shri Balesh Jain, Govindpuri, Haridwar, Shri Pawan Kumar Jain, K-69 Shivalik Nagar and other members.

37.2013-2014 (Panch Kalyanka Silver Jubilee Year) - Two altars were made in the sanctum sanctorum and 'shishpad' was established on one on 22.09.2013. On the other side there will be establishment of 'Dvksh'. The expenses of both the altars were done by Mr. Sanjay Jain, SIDCUL and Mr. Manoj Kumar Jain, Bahadarabad. 'Shishpad' was brought from the funds of the temple and 'Dvksh' was given by Mrs. and Mr. Vijesh Jain, N-210, Shivalik Nagar. On the occasion of Panch Kalyan Silver Jubilee Year, Panchpuri Jain Samaj got a flag printed with Panchangulak and Swastik was distributed as a symbol. Along with this, the Panduk rock and the octagonal skylight on top of the hall, in which rain water used to come, was sanded.
